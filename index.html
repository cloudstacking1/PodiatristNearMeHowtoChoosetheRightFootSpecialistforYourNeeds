What is Podiatrist?

A podiatrist is a medical specialist who diagnoses, treats, and prevents conditions affecting the feet, ankles, and lower limbs. They manage a wide range of issues, including foot pain, injuries, deformities, diabetic foot complications, and nail disorders. Podiatrists provide treatments such as orthotics, physical therapy, medications, and, when necessary, surgical procedures to restore mobility and alleviate discomfort. Their expertise is essential for maintaining foot health, preventing long-term complications, and ensuring overall well-being, especially for individuals with chronic conditions like diabetes or arthritis.

FAQ's

Podiatrists treat a wide range of foot and ankle conditions, including bunions, hammertoes, ingrown toenails, fractures, sprains, and strains. They also provide preventive foot care for individuals with diabetes.

You should consider seeing a podiatrist if you experience persistent foot or ankle pain, notice changes in the appearance of your feet, have an injury, or have a medical condition like diabetes that can affect foot health.

During a podiatry appointment, the podiatrist will take your medical history, perform a physical examination of your feet and ankles, and may order diagnostic tests such as X-rays. Based on the findings, they will discuss a treatment plan tailored to your needs.
